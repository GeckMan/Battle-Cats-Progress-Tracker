Meow Medals are a feature implemented in the Version 9.0 Update. They are achievements earned by completing certain tasks, such as beating Legend Stages or earning cents during battle. Unearned Meow Medals are displayed as grayed-out question mark tokens, except for secret Meow Medals, which just appear as question marks. Secret Meow Medals' names and descriptions are displayed as "???" before they are unlocked, they are unlocked just before beating their respected criteria. The player can view their Meow Medals in the Meow Medals menu, which can be accessed from the Cat Base Menu.

Meow Medals currently serve little purpose other than bragging rights. Their only use is unlocking the last 8 lineups, each of which requires the player to have a specific amount of Medals before it can be purchased using Cat Food:

  • 11th lineup - 25 Meow Medals
  • 12th lineup - 50 Meow Medals
  • 13th lineup - 60 Meow Medals
  • 14th lineup - 70 Meow Medals
  • 15th lineup - 80 Meow Medals
  • 16th lineup - 90 Meow Medals
  • 17th lineup - 100 Meow Medals
  • 18th lineup - 110 Meow Medals

Trivia[]

General[]

  • The puns, references and alliterations that comprise most of the Meow Medals' names are specific to the English Version; the Japanese, Taiwanese and Korean versions go with a much more formal naming scheme, simply using the name of the associated character or stage without any attempt at cleverness. For example, the Legend Cats' Meow Medals just use the names of the units: Forest Child is called Ururun, Tiny Nemesis is called Nyandam, and so on.
    • In BCJP, most of the Cyclone Stages' Meow Medals are named after the Cyclones themselves, but strangely use kanji instead of katakana for their prefixes. For example, the Dark Skies Medal is called 暗黒サイクロン ("Darkness Cyclone") rather than Black Cyclone's Japanese name, ブラックサイクロン (literally "Black Cyclone"). Exceptions include Red Horizon (entirely katakana, just like Red Cyclone's Japanese name) and Typhoon Z (Z Cyclone, whereas Zyclone's Japanese name is Undead Cyclone).
    • Oddly, this isn't the case for all of the Medals bar I Am Uncanny Legend 2 added in Versions 12.3, 12.7 and 13.1, those being Omens, Infernal Tyrant Nyandam, Behemoth Culling, Malevolence and Soractes, which use the same naming scheme as the other versions.
  • The text on the World of Darkness Meow Medal is the Japanese word 魔界 makai ("Demon World" or "Devil World") written in upside-down katakana.
  • Since the Japanese version of Empire of Cats only takes place in Japan instead of the whole world, BCJP has different icons for the Builder of Empires, Undead Slayer and World of Darkness Meow Medals.
    • Moreover, since BCJP only has one set of chapters taking place across the world, the The Future is Ours and ...And Stay Dead Meow Medals lack the UFOs that are present in the English Version, where they are needed to make them distinguishable from the English Empire of Cats Medals.
  • Prior to the addition of Legend Helpers in the Version 10.4 Update, May I Assist You?'s description was, "Recruit a top-class Gamatoto helper."
  • Prior to the reworking of difficulty systems in the Version 10.7 Update, the I Am Legend Meow Medals' descriptions were "Clear all # ★ Stories of Legend maps."
  • When Attack of the Omens was added in Version 12.2, it lacked a Meow Medal unlike the other Colossus Gauntlets. The Medal was later added in Version 12.3.
  • Lord Luza is the only Meow Medal to show a Legend Cat's True Form rather than its Normal Form, most likely because Ancient Egg: N000 is indistinguishable from the other Ancient Eggs.

References[]

  • The I Am Legend Meow Medals reference the 1954 post-apocalypse novel of the same name.
  • Charon's Crossing is a reference to Greek mythology, in which Charon is the ferryman of the underworld who transports souls across the rivers Styx and Acheron.
  • Lock the Gates refers to the fallen gate Rashōmon, which is the namesake of the Advent Stage that must be cleared in order to unlock the Medal.
  • Sanzu Swamp Guardian is a reference to the Sanzu River, a river in Japanese Buddhist tradition which the souls of the deceased must cross before they can reach the afterlife.
  • Holidays in the Sun may be a reference to a song of the same name by the Sex Pistols, especially since the Never Summer! stages' Japanese names are also references to songs.
  • Baron Wasted may be a reference to Baron Wasteland, a character from the game show Where in Time Is Carmen Sandiego?.
  • Who You Gonna Call? is a reference to Ghostbusters.
  • Buns of Steel is a reference to a 90s fitness show of the same name.

Errors[]

  • The Builder of Empires, The Future is Ours and Cosmos Conquistador Medals' descriptions say Gold Treasures where they should say Superior Treasures.
  • Uncanny Legends is not pluralized in the I Am Uncanny Legend Medals' descriptions.
  • Prior to Version 11.4, The Big Z's description said Daboo of the Dead instead of Dead on Debut.
  • Another Year's description refers to Happy New Year? as Happy New Year...?.
  • The "be" in Bears be Bare is capitalized in Unbearable's description.
  • Vox Deus is grammatically incorrect. If referring to Heavenly Herald Papuu, it should instead say Vox Deae which translates to "the voice of a goddess." If it is referring to some other unknown deity, it should instead be Vox Dei.
  • Making Money, Making Money 2, Nouveau Riche, Nouveau Riche 2, One Percenter and One Percenter 2 are ironically unlocked by spending cents, not earning them.
